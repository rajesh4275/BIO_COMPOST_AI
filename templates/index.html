1. The Carbon-Nitrogen Ratio

Maintain a 25:1 to 30:1 C:N balance. Combine "Browns" (Carbon-rich like dried leaves, straw, cardboard) with "Greens" (Nitrogen-rich like food scraps, green clippings).

2. Ideal Moisture Levels

Optimal moisture is 45-60%. It should feel like a damp, wrung-out sponge. If too wet (anaerobic rot, bad odor), mix in dry Browns. If too dry, sprinkle lightly with water.

3. Temperature Zones

Optimal heat is 40°C - 60°C (104°F - 140°F). Heat is generated by microbial activity. If the pile cools down, it's either finished decomposing or needs to be turned.

4. Turn to Aerate

Aerobic bacteria need oxygen to decompose materials without odor. Turn the pile with a fork once or twice a week to inject oxygen and prevent cold pockets.

5. pH Level Insights

Healthy compost has a pH of 6.0 to 8.0. Initial stages are slightly acidic (organic acids forming), but mature compost settles to a neutral or slightly alkaline balance.
